The Reality

This is how most local businesses still operate.

Not because they don't want better — because nothing was built for them.

Notebooks & diaries

Every order, every address, every amount — scribbled in a diary that gets lost, torn, or rained on.

WhatsApp as a CRM

Customer messages buried in 47 group chats. Payment reminders typed out one by one, 20 times a day.

Memory as a database

"Sharma ji ka payment aaya kya?" — nobody knows for sure. Money leaks silently, every single day.

Payments everywhere, visibility nowhere

Cash, UPI, credit, advance — scattered across pockets and apps. No single view of who owes what.

A laundry shop in Pune. Phone, PhonePe QR, and clothes on hangers — but orders still tracked in the owner's head.

Our story

Why we're building this.

SuperLocal didn't start with a pitch deck. It started in laundry shops, tiffin kitchens, and tutoring centres across Maharashtra.

We saw businesses with real demand, loyal customers, and incredible hustle — but almost no operational infrastructure. Orders tracked in notebooks. Payments tracked in memory. Customer communication happening through personal WhatsApp.

Most software today is built for companies with IT teams and training budgets. The tiffin aunty doesn't have an IT team. But she has 80 customers and deserves a system that works for her.

That's what we're building. Not a CRM. Not an ERP. A digital notebook that actually works.
